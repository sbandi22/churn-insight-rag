# SaaS Churn Benchmarks Report (Synthetic Excerpt)

**Source:** Synthetic Industry Benchmark Report, "State of SaaS Retention" (2024)

## Headline Benchmarks

For B2B SaaS companies, a healthy annual logo (customer) churn rate sits between
5% and 7%, with best-in-class companies below 5%. Annual revenue (dollar) churn
is typically lower because expansion revenue from existing accounts offsets
losses; top-quartile companies achieve net revenue retention (NRR) of 110% or
higher. Monthly logo churn above 3% (roughly 30%+ annualized) is considered a
red flag for early-stage products and usually signals product-market-fit or
onboarding problems rather than pricing.

## Churn by Segment

Churn varies sharply by customer size. Self-serve and SMB accounts churn at
3-5% per month, driven by budget sensitivity and low switching costs.
Mid-market accounts churn at roughly 1-2% per month. Enterprise accounts churn
least, often under 0.5% per month, but each loss is far more costly. Enterprise
churn, when it happens, is most often driven by champion turnover (the internal
sponsor leaving), failed value realization, and competitive displacement during
contract renewal rather than price alone.

## Leading Indicators

The strongest leading indicators of SaaS churn are declining product usage
(especially a drop in weekly active users within an account), low feature
adoption breadth, slow time-to-first-value during onboarding, and a rising
volume of unresolved support tickets. Companies that instrument these signals
and trigger proactive customer-success outreach typically reduce gross churn by
15-25% within a year.
