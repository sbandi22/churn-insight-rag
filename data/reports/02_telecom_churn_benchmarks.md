# Telecom Churn Benchmarks Report (Synthetic Excerpt)

**Source:** Synthetic Industry Benchmark Report, "Global Telecom Retention Outlook" (2024)

## Industry Churn Rates

The telecommunications industry has among the highest churn rates of any sector,
with average annual customer churn between 20% and 35% for wireless carriers in
competitive markets. Prepaid mobile churn is substantially higher than postpaid,
frequently exceeding 40% annually, because prepaid customers face no contractual
lock-in. Fixed broadband churn is generally lower, in the 12-18% range, but rises
sharply when promotional pricing periods expire.

## Drivers of Telecom Churn

Price sensitivity is the dominant churn driver in telecom, especially at contract
renewal and at the end of promotional discounts. Network quality and coverage
gaps are the second-largest driver, particularly in rural areas. Other major
factors include poor customer-service experiences (a single unresolved billing
dispute materially raises churn probability), aggressive competitor offers with
device subsidies, and the absence of bundled services. Customers on month-to-month
plans churn far more than those on one- or two-year contracts, and customers
without value-added services such as tech support or online security churn more
than those who adopt them.

## Retention Levers

Effective telecom retention levers include proactive contract-renewal outreach
before promotional pricing lapses, loyalty pricing for tenured customers,
bundling broadband with mobile and streaming, and rapid resolution of first
billing complaints. Carriers that deploy predictive churn scoring to trigger
targeted retention offers report 10-20% reductions in voluntary churn among
high-value postpaid subscribers.
